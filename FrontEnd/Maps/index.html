The Autono-mouse SAE car is a prototype autonomous car built for the racetrack by the UWA Renewable Energy Vehicle (REV) project



2/11

Autono-mouse perceives its environment using sensors such as GPS, lidar and inertial measurement systems



3/11

It can then navigate itself using actuators on the steering wheel and brake, the co-pilot shall manually apply the accelerator



4/11

It has a wealth of safety features, some of which you, as the co-pilot, must control



5/11

Throughout the car, emergency stop (e-stop) switches are used to electrically isolate important circuits
As the co-pilot, you are required to disengage the 'autonomous' and 'drive' switches before driving and engage them in the event of a problem



6/11

To disengage an e-stop, twist the button anti-clockwise until it springs up.
To engage, simply push it in until it locks down.



7/11

The autonomous e-stop is located above the steering wheel



8/11

The drive e-stop is located to the right of the steering wheel



9/11

Before disengaging either e-stop, it is your responsibility to ensure the immediate area is safe
